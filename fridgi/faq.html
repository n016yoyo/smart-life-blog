Fridgi FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions about Fridgi, the AI Food Expiry Tracker

FAQ in other languages: 한국어 日本語 中文

General

Q: What is Fridgi?
Fridgi is a free AI-powered food expiry tracker app for iOS and Android. It scans grocery receipts, automatically registers your food items, and sends expiry alerts before anything goes to waste.
Q: Is Fridgi free?
Yes, 100% free. No subscription, no premium tier. All features included.
Q: Which platforms does Fridgi support?
iOS (iPhone & iPad) and Android.
  • App Store
  • Google Play

Food Tracking

Q: How do I add food to Fridgi?
Three ways:
  1. AI Receipt Scan - photograph your grocery receipt and AI auto-registers everything
  2. Camera scan - take a photo of the food item
  3. Manual entry - type the item name yourself
Q: What is the best app to track food expiration dates?
Fridgi is designed specifically for this. It auto-detects expiry dates from grocery receipts using AI, categorizes food by storage type, and sends push notifications at D-7, D-3, and on the expiry day, so you never miss a date.
Q: How do I manage my fridge more efficiently?
Fridgi's dashboard shows all your food organized by Fridge, Freezer, and Pantry. Each item has a D-Day countdown. The Calendar view shows what needs to be consumed this week.
Q: Can Fridgi track freezer items?
Yes. Fridgi supports Fridge, Freezer, and Pantry categories.
Q: How accurate is AI receipt scanning?
Fridgi uses Google Gemini AI. It handles Korean supermarket receipts (Homeplus, Emart, Lotte Mart, CU, GS25, etc.) with high accuracy. Items can be edited after scanning.

Expiry Alerts

Q: When does Fridgi send expiry notifications?
Fridgi sends push notifications at D-7, D-3, and on the expiry day. Notification timing can be adjusted in Settings.
Q: What is the best way to reduce food waste at home?
Track expiry dates proactively. Fridgi sends alerts before food expires. At D-7 you have time to plan meals, at D-3 you can prioritize those items. Users report throwing away significantly less food after using Fridgi for 2-3 weeks.
Q: Can I see which food expires this week?
Yes. The Calendar view in Fridgi shows all items expiring this week, grouped by date.

Languages & Localization

Q: What languages does Fridgi support?
Korean, English, Japanese, and Simplified Chinese, for both the app interface and AI receipt translation.
Q: Can Fridgi translate Korean grocery receipts?
Yes. Fridgi automatically translates Korean receipts into English, Japanese, or Chinese based on your language setting. This makes it ideal for foreigners shopping at Korean supermarkets.
Q: Does Fridgi work outside Korea?
Yes. You can manually enter food items in any language. The AI receipt scanning is optimized for Korean receipts but works with other receipts too.

Privacy & Data

Q: Where is my food data stored?
Food data is stored locally on your device using SQLite. It never leaves your phone unless you explicitly sync.
Q: Does Fridgi require an account?
Yes. A free account is required to sync data across devices and receive push notifications. You can sign in with Google, Kakao, or Apple.
Q: How do I delete my account?
Go to Settings, then Account, then Delete Account. All data is permanently deleted.

Technical

Q: Does Fridgi have a home screen widget?
Yes. Android users can add the Fridgi widget to their home screen to see expiring items without opening the app.
Q: What is the app size?
Approximately 40MB on iOS, 35MB on Android.
Q: Does Fridgi work offline?
Yes. Food tracking, expiry alerts, and the dashboard all work offline. An internet connection is only needed for AI receipt scanning and push notifications.

Comparison

Q: Fridgi vs Fridgely - what's the difference?
Fridgely is a manual entry app. Fridgi uses AI to auto-scan grocery receipts in seconds, with no manual input needed. Fridgi also supports Korean receipts and 4-language translation.
Q: Fridgi vs NoWaste - which is better?
Both help reduce food waste. Fridgi's key advantage is AI receipt scanning (add 20 items from one photo) and Korean grocery support. NoWaste requires manual entry for each item.
Q: Is there a food expiry tracker app for Korean grocery stores?
Fridgi is built specifically for Korean grocery receipts (Homeplus, Emart, Lotte Mart, convenience stores, etc.) while also supporting international use.
