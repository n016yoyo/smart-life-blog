Korean gift cards (기프티콘, gifticon) expire. And they expire silently — no notification, no reminder, just a dead barcode when you try to use a 50,000 won Starbucks voucher you forgot you had.

The Korean gift card market is massive. Kakao Gift, Naver Pay Gift, GS25, CU, Olive Young — these platforms send out billions of won in gift cards every year. But almost none of them have a good built-in management system. That's where third-party apps come in.

What to Look for in a Gifticon Manager App

Expiry Alerts (Most Important)

This is the whole point. Korean gift cards typically expire in 1-3 years, but many users forget they have them. An app that sends you a push notification 7 days before expiry can save you real money. GiftWallet does this automatically when you scan a card.

AI Barcode Scanning

Manually entering gift card numbers is tedious. The best apps let you point your camera and scan. GiftWallet's AI scanner is fast and accurate with Korean barcodes, including the long numeric codes on Kakao Gift cards.

Multi-Language Support

If you're not reading Korean fluently, most gifticon apps are unusable. GiftWallet is the only dedicated Korean gift card manager with full English support — a significant advantage for expats, tourists, or international workers in Korea.

Offline Access

You need to show the barcode at the counter. Apps that require internet to display a barcode are risky in stores with poor WiFi. GiftWallet stores everything locally.

How Many Gifticons Do Koreans Actually Have?

The average Korean smartphone user receives 15-30 gifticons per year through birthdays, company events, promotions, and random Kakao messages. At an average value of 10,000-50,000 won each, that's a lot of money floating around with expiry dates attached. Most people let at least a few expire every year.
