Fridgi vs Fridgely — Best Food Expiry Tracker App 2026

Published March 2026 · 7 min read

Two apps with almost identical names. Both promise to help you track food expiry dates and reduce food waste. But they take very different approaches — and for most people, one is clearly better.

I used both apps for 30 days, tracking the same groceries in parallel. Here's the full breakdown.

Bottom line: Fridgi wins for simplicity and daily usability. Fridgely has more features but adds friction that reduces how often you actually use it. The best expiry tracker is the one you actually open.

Where Fridgi Wins

Speed of Adding Items

The most important UX metric for a food tracker isn't features — it's how fast you can add something when you're putting away groceries with both hands full. Fridgi's flow is: scan barcode, confirm name, set expiry date, done. Under 10 seconds per item.

Fridgely asks you to categorize items, add storage location, set quantities, and navigate multiple screens. After a big grocery run, this becomes exhausting. The result: people stop using it after a week.

Actually Free

Fridgi has no paywall. Every feature is available from day one with no subscription. Fridgely locks its best features (shopping integration, recipe suggestions) behind a premium tier. For a utility app, paywalling core features is frustrating.

Multi-Language Support

Fridgely is English-only. Fridgi works in English, Korean, Japanese, and Chinese — important for households where English isn't the first language, or for tracking products with Korean/Japanese labeling.

Where Fridgely Wins

Recipe Suggestions

When items are about to expire, Fridgely suggests recipes that use those ingredients. This is a genuinely useful feature — it turns "you need to use this soon" into "here's what to cook tonight." Fridgi doesn't have this.

Shopping List

Fridgely tracks when you've used something and can add it to a shopping list automatically. If you're trying to run your kitchen like a system, Fridgely's approach is more complete.

The Real-World Test

After 30 days, I had logged 67 items in Fridgi and 31 in Fridgely. Not because Fridgi's database was bigger, but because Fridgely's add flow was slow enough that I skipped it for individual items. The app I actually used was better at reducing food waste — even if it had fewer features.

Fridgi also sent more reliable notifications. Fridgely's alerts sometimes came a day late or not at all on iOS 18 (likely a background refresh issue the developer hasn't fixed yet).

Who Should Use Which App

  • Fridgi: Anyone who wants a dead-simple expiry tracker. Families, busy people, anyone who has given up on complex kitchen management apps before. Also the only real option if you need multi-language support.
  • Fridgely: Organized households who want a full kitchen management system and don't mind a learning curve. Better if you cook from scratch regularly and want recipe integration.

The Verdict

Fridgi wins for most people. The best food expiry tracker is the one you actually use daily — and Fridgi's simple UX, fast add flow, and reliable notifications make it the easier habit to maintain. If you've tried and abandoned food tracking apps before, Fridgi is worth another shot.
