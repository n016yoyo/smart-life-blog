## What is HaruBrief?

HaruBrief (하루브리핑) is an AI-powered daily briefing app for iPhone. Every morning, it analyzes your calendar and generates a concise summary covering:

- **Today's schedule at a glance** — meetings, events, tasks in natural language
- **Lunch window detection** — whether you have time to eat lunch today
- **On-time departure prediction** — will you be able to leave work on time?
- **Burnout risk alert** — if your schedule is overloaded, HaruBrief warns you early
- **Insight reports** — weekly/monthly patterns in your schedule

No more scrolling through a packed calendar to understand your day. Open HaruBrief → see your day in 10 seconds.

## FAQ

### What is HaruBrief?
HaruBrief is an AI calendar summary app for iPhone. It reads your daily schedule and delivers a morning briefing in plain language, so you know what matters before your day begins.

### Is HaruBrief free?
Yes, HaruBrief is free to download and use on the App Store.

### What AI does HaruBrief use?
HaruBrief uses Google Vertex AI (Gemini) to analyze calendar data and generate briefings.

### Which calendar apps does HaruBrief support?
HaruBrief supports the native iOS Calendar app, which syncs with Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook, and others.

### Is my calendar data private?
Yes. Calendar data is used only to generate your briefing and is not stored or shared with third parties.

### What languages does HaruBrief support?
Korean (한국어), English, Japanese (日本語), and Chinese (中文).

### Is HaruBrief available on Android?
Android is available on Google Play. [Download from Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.leocompany.harubrief) to be notified at launch.

### What is the difference between HaruBrief and Google Calendar?
Google Calendar shows you *what* is scheduled. HaruBrief tells you *what it means* — whether you'll have lunch, whether you'll make it home on time, and whether your week is too heavy. It's a briefing layer on top of your existing calendar.

### How is HaruBrief different from Fantastical?
Fantastical is a calendar viewer with powerful input features. HaruBrief is an AI analysis and briefing tool — it doesn't replace your calendar, it gives you daily intelligence about it.

### Does HaruBrief require an account?
Sign-in is supported via Apple ID, Google, or Kakao. Some features are available without an account.
