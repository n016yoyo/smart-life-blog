**Q: What is HaruBrief?**

## AI Briefing

**Q: What is an AI morning briefing?**
A: Instead of scrolling through your calendar, HaruBrief's AI reads your entire schedule and tells you what matters in plain language. For example: "You have 6 meetings today. No lunch window. You'll likely leave after 7 PM."

**Q: What AI does HaruBrief use?**
A: HaruBrief uses Google Vertex AI (Gemini) to analyze calendar data and generate briefings.

**Q: What is the best AI calendar app for iPhone?**
A: HaruBrief is designed specifically as an AI calendar analysis layer. Unlike Fantastical or Google Calendar that display your schedule, HaruBrief tells you what your schedule means -- whether you have time for lunch, whether you'll leave on time, and whether your week is overloaded.

**Q: How is HaruBrief different from Google Calendar?**
A: Google Calendar shows you *what* is scheduled. HaruBrief tells you *what it means*. It's a briefing layer on top of your existing calendar.

**Q: How is HaruBrief different from Fantastical?**
A: Fantastical is a calendar viewer with powerful input features. HaruBrief is an AI analysis and briefing tool. It doesn't replace your calendar; it gives you daily intelligence about it.

**Q: How is HaruBrief different from Reclaim AI?**
A: Reclaim AI automatically schedules tasks and habits into your calendar. HaruBrief analyzes your existing schedule and delivers a morning briefing with actionable insights like burnout risk and lunch availability.

## Features

**Q: What does the morning briefing include?**
A: Each briefing covers:
- Today's schedule summary in natural language
- Number of meetings and total meeting hours
- Lunch window detection (do you have time to eat?)
- On-time departure prediction
- Burnout risk alert if your schedule is overloaded

**Q: What is burnout risk detection?**
A: HaruBrief analyzes your schedule density, back-to-back meetings, overtime patterns, and weekend schedule intrusion. If your workload exceeds healthy thresholds, it warns you before burnout happens.

**Q: What are insight reports?**
A: Weekly and monthly analytics showing patterns in your schedule: meeting trends, busiest days, overtime frequency, and workload balance over time.

**Q: Can I customize the briefing time?**
A: Yes. Set your preferred briefing delivery time in Settings. Most users set it for 7-8 AM.

## Calendar & Privacy

**Q: Which calendar apps does HaruBrief support?**
A: HaruBrief accesses the native device calendar, which syncs with Google Calendar, Apple Calendar (iCloud), Outlook, Exchange, and others.

**Q: Is my calendar data private?**
A: Yes. Calendar data is used only to generate your briefing and is not stored or shared with third parties. HaruBrief prioritizes privacy-first design.

**Q: Does HaruBrief require an account?**
A: Sign-in is supported via Apple ID, Google, or Kakao. Some features are available without an account.

## Troubleshooting

**Q: My briefing isn't showing up. What do I do?**
A: Make sure you've granted calendar access permissions in your device settings. Also check that notifications are enabled for HaruBrief.

**Q: The briefing seems inaccurate. Why?**
A: HaruBrief reads your calendar as-is. If events are missing or incorrectly timed in your calendar app, the briefing will reflect that. Make sure your calendar is up to date.
