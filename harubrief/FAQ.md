# HaruBrief — Frequently Asked Questions

## General

**Q: What is HaruBrief?**
A: HaruBrief (하루브리핑) is a free AI morning briefing app for iPhone. Every morning, it analyzes your calendar and tells you your day summary, whether you have time for lunch, whether you'll leave work on time, and your burnout risk level.

**Q: Is HaruBrief free?**
A: Yes — 100% free. No subscription, no premium tier.

**Q: Which platforms does HaruBrief support?**
A: iOS (iPhone). Android version is coming soon — [pre-register on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.leocompany.harubrief).

**Q: What AI does HaruBrief use?**
A: HaruBrief uses Google Vertex AI (Gemini) to analyze calendar data and generate daily briefings.

---

## Calendar & Setup

**Q: Which calendar apps does HaruBrief support?**
A: HaruBrief connects to the native iOS Calendar app, which syncs with Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook, and any other calendar that integrates with iOS.

**Q: Do I need to switch calendar apps?**
A: No. HaruBrief reads your existing iOS calendar — you keep using whatever calendar app you prefer.

**Q: Does HaruBrief require an account?**
A: Sign-in is supported via Apple ID, Google, or Kakao. Some features are available without an account.

---

## Features

**Q: What does the morning briefing include?**
A: Each briefing covers:
- Today's schedule in plain language
- Lunch window detection (is there a free slot?)
- On-time departure prediction (will you leave on time?)
- Burnout risk alert (is your schedule overloaded?)

**Q: What is burnout risk detection?**
A: HaruBrief analyzes your weekly schedule density. When your calendar is consistently overloaded — too many back-to-back meetings, no breaks, late finishes — it flags a high burnout risk so you can adjust before it affects you.

**Q: What are the weekly/monthly insights?**
A: HaruBrief tracks patterns over time: how often you work late, how frequently you miss lunch, your average daily meeting load, and trends week over week.

## Privacy

**Q: Is my calendar data private?**
A: Yes. Calendar data is used only to generate your briefing and is not stored permanently or shared with third parties.

**Q: What data does HaruBrief collect?**
A: HaruBrief reads calendar event titles, times, and durations to generate briefings. It does not read event descriptions, attendee information, or any other personal data. See the full [Privacy Policy](https://harubrief.verification-app.workers.dev/privacy).

## Comparison

**Q: How is HaruBrief different from Google Calendar?**
A: Google Calendar shows you *what is scheduled*. HaruBrief tells you *what it means* — whether you'll have lunch, make it home on time, and whether your week is too heavy. It's an intelligence layer on top of your existing calendar.

**Q: HaruBrief vs Fantastical — which should I use?**
A: They serve different purposes. Fantastical is a calendar *viewer and input* tool. HaruBrief is a calendar *analysis* tool that briefs you each morning. Many users use both. See the [full comparison](https://harubrief-blog.pages.dev/en/blog/harubrief-vs-fantastical/).

**Q: HaruBrief vs Reclaim AI?**
A: Reclaim AI auto-schedules tasks into your free time. HaruBrief analyzes your calendar and tells you what your day looks like. Different use cases; can be used together.

**Q: Is there an AI calendar app for Android?**
A: HaruBrief for Android is coming soon. [Pre-register on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.leocompany.harubrief) to be notified at launch.
